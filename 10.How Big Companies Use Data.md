# Day 10 — How Big Companies Use Data

Big companies depend on data to make fast, accurate, and smart decisions.
Everything they do — marketing, product design, pricing, customer experience — is powered by data.

Today we look at **how major companies around the world use data to stay successful**.

---
![image alt](Image/HowBigCompaniesUseData.jpg)

# 1. **Netflix — Personalized Experience**

Netflix uses huge amounts of data to understand what people like to watch.

They collect:

* What movies you watch
* When you stop or pause
* What genres you prefer
* How long you watch
* Your rating and reactions

They use this data to:

### Recommend shows

Each user sees a different homepage.

### Decide which shows to produce

If many people watch crime dramas → Netflix invests more in crime shows.

### Improve thumbnails

Netflix tests many thumbnails and chooses the one that gets the most clicks.

**Data = better engagement, more subscribers.**

---

# 2. **Spotify — Music Recommendations**

Spotify tracks:

* Songs you listen to
* How long you listen
* Songs you skip
* Your mood playlists
* Your most played artists

They use data to create:

* Discover Weekly
* Daily Mix
* Personalized playlists

This keeps users listening longer.

Spotify also uses data to:

* Predict upcoming hit songs
* Suggest music based on time of day
* Help artists understand their audience

---

# 3. **Amazon — Product Recommendations & Pricing**

Amazon is a **data-driven machine**.

They use data for:

### Product recommendations

“Customers who bought this item also bought…”

### Dynamic pricing

Prices change based on:

* Demand
* Competition
* Time of day
* Stock levels

### Inventory management

Predicting which items will run out soon.

### Fast delivery planning

Data helps Amazon decide:

* Where to place warehouses
* How to reduce delivery time

Amazon wouldn’t work without data.

---

# 4. **TikTok — The For You Page Algorithm**

TikTok collects:

* What you like
* What you skip
* How long you watch each video
* Your comments
* What you share

TikTok uses this to build a super personalized **For You Page (FYP)**.

### Keeps you watching longer

The more you watch, the better TikTok understands you.

### Finds trends

TikTok identifies viral content early.

### Helps creators grow

Creators get suggested to the right audience.

---

# 5. **Football Clubs — Player Analytics**

Modern football clubs use data to improve performance.

They track:

* Speed
* Distance covered
* Pass accuracy
* Heat maps
* Shot probability

Clubs use this data to:

* Decide player positions
* Build match strategies
* Identify strong/weak areas
* Buy new players
* Prevent injuries

Teams using data win more games.

Example: Liverpool FC uses advanced analytics for recruitment.

---

# 6. **Uber / Bolt — Pricing & Matching**

Ride apps use real-time data to:

### Match drivers with riders

Closest driver gets the ride.

### Predict demand

If many people need rides → more drivers sent to the area.

### Surge pricing

Prices increase when demand is high (rain, traffic, rush hour).

### Reduce waiting time

Data helps balance supply and demand.

---

# 7. **Banks — Fraud Detection & Risk Management**

Banks analyze:

* Spending patterns
* Login activity
* Location data
* Transaction history

If something unusual happens:
 The system detects fraud instantly.

Banks also use data to:

* Approve loans
* Predict credit risk
* Recommend financial products

---

# 8. **Hospitals — Patient Safety & Predictions**

Hospitals use data to:

* Predict which patients need urgent care
* Manage medicine and equipment
* Track treatment results
* Reduce waiting time

Data saves lives.
