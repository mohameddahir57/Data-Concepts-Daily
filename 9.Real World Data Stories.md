# Day 9 — Real World Data Stories

Today we explore **how real companies and industries use data** in everyday life.
These examples show why data analysis is powerful and important.

Real-world stories make learning easier and help you understand how your future skills will be used.

---

# **1. Supermarket: Understanding Customer Buying Behavior**

Supermarkets collect data from:

* Receipts
* Loyalty cards
* Inventory systems

They use this data to answer:

* Which products are most popular?
* What time of day has the highest sales?
* Which promotions increase revenue?

**Data example insight:**
  *“People who buy diapers often also buy baby wipes.”*
This helps them place products together to increase sales.

---

# **2. Netflix: Personalized Recommendations**

Netflix uses data from:

* What you watch
* How long you watch
* What you pause
* What you skip
* What you search

They use this to:

* Recommend new movies
* Choose which shows to produce
* Create personalized thumbnails

**Data example insight:**
  *“Viewers in Kenya like action movies more on weekends.”*

This helps Netflix decide what content to show each user.

---

# **3. TikTok: Video Recommendations and Engagement**

TikTok collects data about:

* Videos you like
* Videos you skip quickly
* What you comment
* How long you watch each video

They use this data to build your **For You Page (FYP)**.

**Data example insight:**
  *“Users who watch sports videos also enjoy motivational clips.”*

This makes your FYP feel tailor-made.

---

# **4. Football: Player Performance and Match Strategy**

Football teams analyze:

* Pass accuracy
* Speed
* Distance covered
* Goal probability
* Opponent behavior

This helps coaches decide:

* Which players to start
* Where to focus training
* How to defend against strong opponents

**Data example insight:**
  *“When a striker runs from the left side, goal probability increases by 20%.”*

Teams with strong data analysis win more games.

---

# **5. Uber / Bolt: Pricing and Driver Matching**

Ride-hailing apps use data to:

* Match drivers with riders
* Estimate ride prices
* Reduce waiting time
* Predict busy areas

**Data example insight:**
  *“At 6 PM, demand in the city center is extremely high.”*

This triggers **surge pricing** and increases the number of available drivers.

---

# **6. Hospitals: Predicting Patient Needs**

Hospitals use data to:

* Track patient history
* Predict risks
* Improve treatment plans
* Manage equipment and staff

**Data example insight:**
 *“Patients with certain symptoms may need urgent attention within 2 hours.”*

Data saves lives.

---

# **7. Banks: Fraud Detection**

Banks analyze:

* Transaction patterns
* Spending behavior
* Login locations

If something unusual happens, the system flags it.

**Data example insight:**
  *“Customer usually buys in Mogadishu, but today a transaction came from another country.”*

The bank blocks the card automatically for safety.

---

# **8. Online Stores: Product Recommendations**

E-commerce platforms (Amazon, Alibaba, Shopify) use data to:

* Recommend products
* Set prices
* Track inventory
* Identify trends

**Data example insight:**
  *“Customers who buy phones also buy phone cases.”*

This increases sales through **cross-selling**.
